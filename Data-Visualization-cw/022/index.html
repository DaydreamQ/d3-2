About

About Boston

Boston is the capital and most populous city of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts in the United States, and one of the 21st most populous city in the world.

Topic of the Analysis

This project is an exploration of residents’ educational attainment in Boston, which is conducted from three different dimensions: age, sex and labor force.

Aim of the Analysis

The student attempts to present this analysis to help people get a better understanding of the differences in educational attainment among Boston residents.




Graph



By Age

Analysis:

This figure shows the difference of educational attainment between five age groups of Boston residents. It can be concluded that the population in the age of "45-64" takes the most part of the total. By comparing these age levels, we can see that only small part of citizens do not graduate from high school while most people own a bachelor degree or even higher. The high-school-graduated people and those who have attended some college are basically at same level in four age groups except the group of '25-34'.

By Sex

Analysis:

This figure shows the difference of educational attainment between male and female. It can be seen easily that the people who stop studying after graduated from high school stays at the highest level, which followed by the number of people with bachelor degree. The difference of educational attainment between men and women is quite small except the level of 'associated degree', where the number of women is more than twice as much as that of men.


By Labor Force

Analysis:

This figure shows the difference of educational attainment between employed and unemployed people. There are around 94% of the citizens are employed while the people who don't have jobs take very small part of the whole population. The number of people who own a bachelor degree or higher is quite large (about 1.4 million). The groups of 'high school graduate' and 'some college education' are stay at similar levels. Only few residents state that they never received education above high school. In addition, there are very small fluctuations in different level of educational attainment among the unemployed residents.
